Areas of interest: combinatorics, graph theory

I am a PhD candidate in mathematics at the University of Vermont, advised by Professor Puck Rombach. I recieved my Bachelors in 2017 in Mathematics and English. For a year, I taught middle school English language courses near Nantes, France. Over the course of my PhD, I have been a graduate student instructor, a graduate research assistant, and now a graduate fellow for the Vermont Space Grant Consortium.

My research primarily concerns graph theory, including extremal and algebraic problems, though I do some matroid theory too.

Workshops

Emléktábla 15th Emléktábla Workshop in Vác, Hungary, July 2024. I participated in the Extremal combinatorics group.

MASI Masamu Advanced Study Institute and Workshops (virtual in 2020 and 2021, Pretoria, South Africa in 2023). I participated each time in the graph theory work group, meeting wonderful people and finding good problems to work on. The 2020 and 2021 workshops lead to the papers Toughness of recursively partitionable graphs and On the last new vertex visited by a random walk in a directed graph. A project from the 2023 workshop is underway.

GRWC Graduate Research Workshop in Combinatorics (virtual in 2021, Denver, CO in 2022). I had the pleasure of working on a number of problems suggested by my fellow graduate students and of leading working groups on odd covers of graphs with bicliques in 2021 and on odd covers with paths and cycles in 2022.
